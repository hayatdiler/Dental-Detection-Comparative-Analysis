Tooth Detection in Panoramic Radiographs: A Comparative Analysis of RT-DETR, YOLOv8-L, and Faster R-CNN ArchitecturesThis project compares the performance of modern deep learning architectures for the automated detection and classification of teeth in panoramic radiographs, a fundamental diagnostic tool in dentistry.👥 AuthorsEmine Umay KILIÇ - Marmara University, Computer EngineeringMuhammed Enes YILDIRIR - Marmara University, Computer EngineeringHayat DİLER - Marmara University, Computer Engineering📝 Project AbstractManual tooth detection is time-consuming and prone to errors due to clinician fatigue. This study aims to automate this process and enhance clinical reliability by evaluating RT-DETR, YOLOv8-L, and Faster R-CNN architectures.📊 Dataset SpecificationsScope: A total of 968 panoramic X-ray images from patients of various age groups.Labeling: Meticulously labeled by dental experts in accordance with FDI (Fédération Dentaire Internationale) standards.Classes: 8 main classes (Incisor, Canine, Premolar, Molar, and their deciduous counterparts).Methodology: Data augmentation techniques such as horizontal flipping, brightness adjustment, and rotation were applied to address class imbalance.🛠️ Technical Details & TrainingModels were trained in an environment optimized for high computational efficiency:Framework: PyTorch & UltralyticsHardware: NVIDIA GPU & CUDA 11.8Parameters: 50 Epochs, 4 Batch Size, 640x640 Image SizeOptimizers: AdamW for YOLOv8 and RT-DETR; SGD for Faster R-CNN🏆 Performance ComparisonThe general mAP@0.5 success rates achieved during the experiments are as follows:ArchitectureSuccess Rate (mAP@0.5)Key CharacteristicsYOLOv8-L94.6%Highest overall accuracy and detection stability.RT-DETR92.1%Global context processing and balanced error distribution.Faster R-CNN77.95%High accuracy in permanent teeth, performance drop in deciduous teeth.Note: The YOLOv8-L architecture demonstrated a detection success rate of over 98% specifically for permanent tooth classes, proving its clinical reliability.🖼️ Visual ResultsPrediction outputs and error analyses (Confusion Matrices) were examined in detail:YOLOv8-L: Bounding boxes were found to be positioned closer to the actual tooth contours.RT-DETR: Captured the relative positions of teeth on the dental arch more consistently.Faster R-CNN: Overlapping boxes were observed in regions where teeth are closely packed.📚 ReferencesThis study was developed based on key research in the literature:Temur, K.
